# Three minute video script for the Maitri submission

Target length: one hundred and eighty seconds. Three act structure. Plain spoken voiceover, screen capture on a calm cursor, no music sting.

## Act one. The problem. 0:00 to 0:35

Open on the words on screen: one woman every two minutes. Hold three seconds.

Voiceover begins.

"In two thousand and twenty three, two hundred and sixty thousand women died from pregnancy. One every two minutes. Most of them in places like Saharsa district in Bihar, India, where the nearest functioning maternal care facility can be fifteen kilometres away and the frontline is a community health worker with a paper card and a low end phone."

Cut to a slow zoom of the Saharsa map or a generic rural India street if no licensed footage is available. Pixabay and Pexels both have free B roll. Avoid stock photos that look staged.

Voiceover continues.

"What if she had decision support that actually fails safe."

## Act two. The hero loop. 0:35 to 2:15

Cut to the Maitri Gradio interface on screen. The patient snapshot for the hero case is already filled. The demo mode toggle is visible and on.

Voiceover.

"This is Maitri. Seven agents, two Gemma 4 models. Watch what happens."

Click Run Maitri. While the panels fill, voiceover continues over the loading state.

"The Specialist drafts a risk assessment with cited claims."

Pause until the initial draft panel renders. Highlight one cited evidence chunk identifier with the cursor.

Voiceover.

"Now the independent Verifier checks every claim against the evidence."

The red verifier banner appears. Hold cursor on it for two seconds.

Voiceover.

"It catches an unsupported claim about a specific magnesium sulphate dose that the evidence does not back. The system stops. The Optimizer writes a rewrite hint."

Cursor moves down to the optimizer hint panel.

Voiceover.

"The Specialist runs again and the second draft passes. Only then does the Referral and Facility agent pick a destination."

The referral panel renders. Cursor on the facility name.

Voiceover.

"Saharsa District Hospital. Open, capable of obstetric surgery, ambulance available. Not the closer primary health centre because that one does not have the capability this case needs."

Cursor moves down.

Voiceover.

"And here is the message for the mother in Hindi and a separate one for the household decision maker, with the voucher she is eligible for named directly."

Brief hold on the audit summary at the bottom.

Voiceover.

"Every call is in an append only audit log. Eight entries for this case."

## Act three. The vision. 2:15 to 3:00

Cut to a black slate with the words on screen: precision one, recall one, six adversarial cases.

Voiceover.

"On a hand crafted adversarial set, the verifier caught five out of five planted hallucinations and accepted the one clean case. The deterministic safety rule block passes its full unit suite."

Cut to a slate of the architecture diagram. Hold three seconds.

Voiceover.

"Two Gemma 4 models, seven agents, six native function calls, one verifier loop. Apache licensed. Public live demo. Public repository."

Cut to a final slate. The words: a safety verified Gemma 4 maternal referral co pilot for every community health worker.

Voiceover.

"Maitri. A verified co pilot for the woman who cannot wait."

Fade to logo or to a still frame of the verifier rejection moment.

End at 3:00.

## Production checklist

- Screen capture at one thousand four hundred and forty by nine hundred pixels minimum, twenty four frames per second.
- Voiceover recorded with a basic USB microphone in a small room, no echo.
- Captions burned in, English captions throughout. The Hindi messages on screen do not need English captions because the on screen text speaks for itself.
- Music optional. If used, royalty free instrumental at sixteen decibels below the voiceover.
- No emojis on screen. No decorative symbols. Plain typography.

## What to film if you want a face

A real or proxy community health worker, fifteen seconds in act one, holding a phone with the Maitri interface open. The face turns the abstract two minute mortality stat into a person. This is the single highest leverage shot you can add and the only one I cannot generate.

If you cannot film a person, an alternative is a still frame from a public photograph licensed under Creative Commons of an Indian ASHA worker in the field, attributed in the writeup.
